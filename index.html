01 About

I am the Founder & CEO of Maxora AI, building AI products and research-driven technology. My research applies meta-learning and exploration to natural language generation — bridging GANs, diffusion models, and meta reinforcement learning.

A single idea threads through my work: a scheduler / explorer–exploiter framework inspired by Meta Exploration. Rather than hand-tuning how a model samples or schedules noise, I train a second network to learn that policy — and let the generator focus on generation.

02 How my methods work

NeurIPS 2024

Meta-DiffuB

A contextualized seq2seq text diffusion model with meta-exploration.

code ↗

A fixed noise schedule treats every sentence the same. Meta-DiffuB trains a Scheduler (the meta-explorer) that reads the context and imposes a contextualized, per-step noise schedule on the diffusion model (the exploiter). Generation quality becomes the reward that teaches the scheduler how to noise.

MetaEx-GAN

Meta exploration to improve NLG via GANs.

code ↗

GAN-based text generation collapses when rewards are sparse and exploration is poor. MetaEx-GAN adds a Meta-Explorer that learns an exploration policy for the Generator, while the Discriminator’s signal flows back as reward — an explorer–exploiter loop that escapes mode collapse.
